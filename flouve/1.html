les égouts de lausanne, suisse

lausanne is a ridiculously easy place to live, especially on beautiful clear sun soaked days. forests, alps, lakes, castles, weird metro hardly controlled, junkies get free clean heroin, rich folk get shiny cars and mansions, military jet planes take off from underground mountain airstrips, lake geneva shines and sparkles thick with lead, cadmium, zinc and poop. poop gets treated underground just like their military air strips, and its very likely the building is topped with evergreen trees. but how did this ridiculously pretty little swiss city end up this way? history tells us back in 1832 there was a cholera breakout. history also told curly two rivers used to pass through the city. where do you think this curlybean slopped deep and dark as soon as the weather came crystal clear?





la louve

the louve enters lausanne city hidden behind a large bus depot, recognizable by a great sewer stench, cold and humid pushing through the upper forests tickling the lustful nose of such excitable humans as me. curlybean was willing to go all night manhole popping to get my way into the buried river as always, but it wasnt even necessary. little switzerland just warns you by way of a small, not even passive aggressive, well worded sign demonstrating that the stream floods rapidly. well shucks. looks like this curly is heading straight underground this fine day. but how far underground?



lausanne is STEEP.

this first day, i went on an incredibly brainbending poopy journey. unparalleled. more terrifying than buried rivers over in its just as deafening-massive-torrent unfit for human thoroughfare tunnel sister city, oslo. poopy as the poopiest of the st paul sewers. bendier than a confused steep sydney stormwater drain. but, i mean, where had i ever seen staircases like these before? maybe twin cities? (helixes!)
nowhere.
especially when you have struggled to find footing down the (youve lost count how many) ochre stained calcite patterned poop glazed stairwells, no buried river nor combined sewer or storm drain really sizes up.





grande escalier de la bouclette

so there you are. you came across a cute old black and white photo of a perfectly curved junction and more than half a dozen folk standing proud of their feat, taming the louve and flon, close to two hundred years of work engineering one of the steepest, deepest drainage systems in the world... and here i am, clutching a short bundle of shitty rope and a temperamental flashlight. barely able to keep a hold of the rusted railings, i felt the combination of my non existent expectations prior to going head first into the louve and now being confronted with serious as life situations is definitely reason to find an exit, and reconsider how i fit into the picture.





la louve reaches a hard turn under a buried bridge, high veocity.


yes. the ledges you once slid about at a comfortable height above the rapid shit torrent running along the centre of the tunnnnel are now crumblng exponentially with each sweaty palmed step. the railing you once clutched hard onto is long rusted off and into the very shit torrent you are avoiding yourself. this sharp bend marked curlybeans breaking point.

up a decent staircase off the main tunnel. the waiting game begins. one eye poked through the clogged eye of the manhole, absolutely no chance to hear road traffic or pedestrians - even up here the endless noise of the high velocity shit fest back in main tunnel cancels out anything above you. a quarter hour maybe? one person passes over the lid. or was that a car? fuck!

shit mist infused sweat beads from head to toe, no more considering, just a whole bunch of doing. moment of truth.



curly forgets the lens length of cam cam in the heat of the moment

well shit! turned out to be a ramp leading into an underground carpark! and what is that? two guards waiting for me as i climb out? damn. wait! they are smiling! frangais is now in full effect. random words and broken sentences splurted through gritty teeth "oui! cest sublime, tres jolie - louve? oui a la!" ...the guards kept a safe distance from me and the stench id unhatched from their turf but acted quite curious and shocked. all in all a lovely run in with guards when i compare to previous less smiley more brutal confrontations.



next up: le flon (the other buried river)

les égouts de lausanne, suisse



le flon

so, the first unprepared attempt to reach lausannes buried confluence of the louve and flon ended me up hitting reverse in the depths of it. what i eventually found out by head diving down the terrifyingly mammoth louve-flon system from its other buried river (the flon) is that i was actually only about 100m from the junction the day before.

the flon heads towards lausanne city from behind a huge shiny new incinerator. large sewer overflows roar within its first two long deep slides. the atmosphere in this buried river is very different to the louve as a strong cold air current sucks down any shit mist from the slides down into the belly of the city faster than you could bare try follow. in fact, this air current was so strong that finally taking a moment to think and get my heart beating a calmer pace at the bottom of the second slide here:



a moment of hesitation hit with the assumption the current had grown since entering the mouth of the beast. not so. this thing just pulls all matter downwards at an alarming rate. ahead from this stretched out slide the flon remains concrete arched through until a long long while it finally takes a similar shape/material as the louve.

so much so, when the two finally meet it is almost a mirror image.
lausanne confluence





the original black and white photograph that got me obsessed in the first place

dear drain folk, please forgive me. the modern photo of the junction is not taken by me. id credit the local who took it if i knew who the credit was due to. by this point (believe me, its a LONG way to follow the full underground course of the flon to this section) all my shit was falling apart. batteries drained, body aching - all the fun stuff. unfortunately i hadnt found any exits like the day before. i slogged back uphill, giddy although ruined by the whole ordeal and finally slipped out the upper mouth once more.
