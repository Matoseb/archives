I've thought of that in the past... the problem with most drains would be the irregular flow. Unless it's a stream or something.

If one lived above a major drain/sewer, you could potentially get free power by dropping a stream turbine into the flow

http://www.absak.c...9_90/products_id/3

Obviously the problem with a major sewer or even a drain is debris buildup. At best it would clog or damage the turbine, at worst, the turbine would act as a blockage and cause enough debris to build up to flood the system. Even if that didn't happen, in systems with just enough flow/slope to keep things moving, a turbine would tend to slow down the flow and possibly cause silt to build up.
